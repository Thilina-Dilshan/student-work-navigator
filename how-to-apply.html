How to Apply – Step by Step

A simple process can make a big difference. Follow these five steps from search to follow-up.

1

Find a Suitable Job

Start broad, then narrow down. Decide which job type fits your visa status, schedule and goals (see Job Types), then search using our Resources page.

  • Check the OVGU job portal and career service board first — postings there are pre-vetted for students.
  • Set up alerts on LinkedIn and general job boards using keywords like “Werkstudent”, “HiWi”, or “Minijob” + Magdeburg.
  • Ask classmates, tutors and your international office — many roles are filled through word of mouth before they're even posted.
2

Prepare Your Documents

German employers expect a complete, well-formatted application. Prepare these in advance so you can apply quickly when you find a good match.

  • CV (Lebenslauf): 1–2 pages, reverse-chronological, with a professional photo (common in Germany, though increasingly optional).
  • Cover letter (Anschreiben): tailored to the specific role, explaining why you and why this company.
  • Enrollment certificate (Immatrikulationsbescheinigung) and, if asked, transcripts or a copy of your residence permit.

See the full CV & Application Guide for templates and examples.

3

Apply

Submit carefully and on time — small details matter to German employers.

  • Follow the application method requested exactly (online form, email, or portal upload).
  • Name your files clearly, e.g. Lastname_CV.pdf, Lastname_CoverLetter.pdf.
  • Double-check contact details, dates and that documents are attached as PDFs, not editable files.
  • Apply promptly — many student positions are filled on a rolling basis.
4

Prepare for Interview

Interviews for student jobs are usually shorter and less formal than full-time interviews, but preparation still counts.

  • Research the company/department and be ready to explain your motivation and availability (hours per week, semester breaks).
  • Prepare answers about your study progress, German/English level, and relevant coursework or projects.
  • Prepare 2–3 questions to ask them — about the team, tasks, or flexibility around exam periods.
  • If it's on campus, arrive early; if remote, test your camera/microphone beforehand.
5

Follow Up

Staying professional after the interview leaves a good impression — and helps you keep track of multiple applications.

  • Send a short thank-you email within 1–2 days if you don't hear back on a decision date.
  • Keep a simple tracker (spreadsheet) of where you applied, when, and the current status.
  • If rejected, it's fine to politely ask for brief feedback — many recruiters are happy to share a tip or two.
  • Once you accept an offer, confirm your start date, weekly hours, and required paperwork in writing.
